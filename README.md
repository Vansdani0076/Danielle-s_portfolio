# 🌙 Sleep Health & Lifestyle Analysis

### Data Analytics Portfolio Project

## 📌 Project Overview

Does a high-activity lifestyle actually guarantee better sleep? This project analyzes the complex relationship between sleep patterns, daily stress, and physical health markers. Using a dataset of 374 individuals, I explored how variables like occupation and BMI category act as leading indicators for sleep quality and duration.

The goal was to move beyond basic correlations and identify the specific "tipping points" where lifestyle factors (like stress levels) begin to statistically degrade sleep health.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

  * **Data Visualization:** Power BI (Interactive Dashboards)
  * **Data Processing:** Microsoft Excel / CSV
  * **Documentation:** Microsoft Word ([D213 Task 2.docx](https://github.com/Vansdani0076/Danielle-s_portfolio/blob/main/D213%20Task%202.docx))

## 📊 Key Insights & Results

Based on the analysis visualized in the [Sleep Viz.pdf](https://github.com/Vansdani0076/Danielle-s_portfolio/blob/main/Sleep%20Viz.pdf), here are the primary findings:

  * **The BMI Quality Gap:** My analysis revealed that individuals in the **"Normal" BMI category** maintain a significantly higher average sleep quality compared to those in the "Overweight" or "Obese" categories.
  * **Stress as a Primary Driver:** There is a sharp "quality cliff" at a Stress Level of 7/10; beyond this point, sleep quality drops by over 20% regardless of total sleep duration.
  * **Occupational Trends:** Healthcare professionals (Nurses/Doctors) showed the highest variance in sleep consistency, highlighting the impact of shift-based work on circadian health.
